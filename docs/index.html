General Help

The purpose of this guide is to help explain some of the deeper mechanics of Spellcraft. Note that information in tooltips of items will not be displayed here.

Spellcraft Progression

Your first step to becoming a wizard is to create a Mana Stone.
After that, you need to craft an Altar of the same color as the spell you want to make.

Altar GUI:
Mana Stone Slot: Placing a Mana Stone here will recharge mana over time. The more bookshelves around the altar, the faster mana will regenerate.
Mana Regeneration Status: This box will show the current rate of mana regeneration.
Empty Tome Slot: Place empty spell tomes for crafting here.
Secondary Ingredient Slot: Place secondary spell ingredients here. You can find the list of spells in-game by right clicking the tomes, or through the appropriate help pages for each class.
Craft Spell Button: When a valid recipe is in the altar, you can press this button to begin the spell crafting process. This button will also specify what spell will be crafted before you press it.
Progress Bar: This bar will show the progress of the spell that is currently being crafted.
Result Slot: Finished spells will go here when crafting is complete.
Once you have the spell tomes, its just a matter of right-clicking with the tome to cast it!
Spells can be further improved using some of the other mechanics mentioned below.

Spell Level Mechanics

Many spells will gain experience when cast. When enough experience is gained, the spell will level up, and become more powerful.
You can freely toggle through the unlocked levels of a spell by sneaking and right-clicking the tome.

Class Specialization and Class Level Mechanics

Spellcraft consists of 8 unique classes of magic. They work in sets of 2, countering the other. The mechanics of these classes will be explained below.

Spellcraft's Classes: (Organized by set, Click any of the classes for more information)
VermillionShadow
AmberineCrimson
VerdantAmaranthine
AzureIridescent

These classes determine your maximum mana as well as certain spells' effectiveness.
Maximum mana can be calulated by adding 10x every class level you have, plus 100 base mana.

In Spellcraft, you start as a Universal Wizard. You may choose to specialize in any of the 8 available classes to gain benefits in that color.
Should you choose to do so, simply use your Mana Stone on the appropriate altar of the color you choose.
When you do this, spells of the color you chose will get better. The effect can vary from reduced mana cost, stronger effect, reduced cooldown, etc.
If you do choose a color, however, the opposite color's spells will become worse/harder to use.

When casting a spell belonging to a certain class, you will gain experience in that class equal to the mana spent to cast the spell.
When enough experience is gained, you will level up in the class, and gain more maximum mana and spells that scale with the class may get better.

Special Item Info

Here you can find any special information about generic items.

Mana Stone Regenerates 1% of maximum mana every 10 seconds. Gem changes color with chosen class, and base can be colored by crafting with dye.
Chalice of Harmony Improves mana regeneration by reducing the time to regenerate mana by 5 seconds.
Uses 20 mB/regen tick of stored mana potion, craft with mana potions to add more.
Can hold a max of 8 potions.
Current mana will be blue to indicate this effect on the mana HUD.
